Streching the springs

Rebuilding the geometric ideal system's behavior using algebraic symbols

Now that we understand spring force, let us construct using symbols the force that both the stretched springs will exert on \(m_{i}\). Let:
\( F_{i-1}( x_{i})\) = Force exerted by the \(m_{i-1}\) spring on \(m_{i}\)
\( F_{i+1}( x_{i})\) = Force exerted by the \(m_{i+1}\) spring on \(m_{i}\)

Drag the slider to transition from an unstretched string to a stretched string.

As you can see, stretching changes the vertical distance between \(m_{i-1}\) & \(m_{i}\) from 0 to AB. Therefore, amount of stretch
           =\( AB\)
          \(=\ Y_{x_{i-1}} \ -\ Y_{x_{i}}\)

Therefore, \( F_{i-1}( x_{i})\) \(=\ k_{1}( Y_{x_{i-1}} \ -\ Y_{x_{i}})\)
Similarly, \( F_{i+1}( x_{i})\) \(=\ k_{1}( Y_{x_{i+1}} \ -\ Y_{x_{i}})\)
